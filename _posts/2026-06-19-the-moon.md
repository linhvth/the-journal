*"I thought I was looking at the moon, but it turned out to be just a streetlight."*

There have been many moments in my life when I believed I had found something extraordinary. It can be an opportunity, a dream, a place, or a person. Something that seemed to glow with a special kind of light, like a serene moon. But as time passed and the fog slowly lifted, I realized that what I was seeing wasn't necessarily special. More often than not, it was my longing reflected back at me. 

I wanted it so badly that I mistook it for something greater than it was.

I romanticized it. I projected meaning onto it. I built stories around it and attached it to hopes, dreams, and identities that it never actually carried. The thing itself was often ordinary. It was my imagination that made it luminous.

In a way, I wasn't seeing it at all. I was seeing my own desire.
